About me

Hannah Donovan is an entrepreneur, product/design leader, and public speaker. She’s been working in entertainment tech for over a decade. 

Hannah is the founder and CEO of an early stage VC-backed company called TRASH, building predictive editing for video using computer vision. Before that she was the General Manager at Twitter-owned Vine; led the team and product at Expa-backed music startup Drip (acquired by Kickstarter); was VP Design at Ripcord (a product incubator funded by MTV); co-founded the song-sharing service This Is My Jam while leading R&D design for The Echo Nest (acquired by Spotify) and was part of the original leadership team at early music streaming service Last.fm (acquired by CBS) where she led design.

She’s spoken at numerous conferences worldwide and lectured at top universities including Imperial College London, Princeton, Parsons, and as faculty (visiting lecturer) at Cornell Tech. Her work has been featured by media such as the Guardian, the New York Times, Forbes, Variety, and the Today Show.

She’s also a startup advisor, and frequently mentors others through programs including Google’s 30 Weeks, First Round Capital’s Product Program. She’s passionate about helping change the ratio in tech for under-represented people. Han writes an occasional newsletter on entrepreneurship, and co-hosts a weekly podcast on being a founder called It Just Got Real.

Han currently lives in Los Angeles. She’s also an cellist and in her free time crate-digs for hiphop tracks. She publishes a monthly playlist of new music called SISTERS, and in real life she has five.
